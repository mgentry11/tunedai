The Process

How fine-tuning works.

We take an existing open-source model and train it further on examples specific to your domain. The result is a model that thinks like an expert in your field — at a fraction of the cost of a large closed-source API.

Step 01

Define your task

We start with the specific reasoning task you need the model to excel at — whether that's legal analysis, medical diagnosis support, financial modeling, or anything else that requires precise, domain-specific thinking.

Step 02

Build the training set

We curate or generate high-quality examples of the task done correctly. This is where most fine-tuning fails — we specialize in building training data that actually teaches the model to reason, not just pattern-match.

Step 03

Fine-tune and verify

We train the model using efficient techniques (LoRA) that work on modest hardware. We measure the result on a held-out benchmark before and after — so you see exactly what changed, not just our claim that it improved.

Why Open Source

Own your model.
Own your costs.

Every API call to a closed-source model is a recurring cost you don't control, on infrastructure you don't own, with a provider who can change pricing, deprecate your model, or go down. Fine-tuned open-source models eliminate all of that.

💰

Dramatically lower cost

A fine-tuned 7B model running on your own hardware costs a fraction of a GPT-4o API call. At scale, the difference is orders of magnitude.

🔒

Your data stays private

No API call means no data leaving your environment. Critical for healthcare, legal, finance, and defense applications where data cannot touch a third-party server.

⚡

No vendor dependency

OpenAI deprecates models. Anthropic changes pricing. Google pivots. A fine-tuned model you own runs the same way forever, regardless of what any vendor decides.

🎯

Better at your task

A general-purpose model is trained to do everything adequately. A fine-tuned model is trained to do your specific task exceptionally. The benchmark above shows what that difference looks like.
